Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026

Worldwide · Includes GDPR (EEA/UK) and other applicable privacy protections.

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Towly (“Towly,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal information when you use the Towly mobile application and related services (the “App” or “Service”).

Towly is offered worldwide. This Policy is intended to meet requirements under the EU/EEA General Data Protection Regulation (“GDPR”), the UK GDPR, the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection, the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the CPRA (“CCPA”), and other applicable privacy laws.

By using the Service, you acknowledge this Policy. If you do not agree, do not use the Service.

2. Who we are (data controller)

For personal data processed in connection with the Service, the data controller is the operator of Towly, reachable at support@towly.app.

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you may contact us at the same address to exercise your rights or raise privacy questions. Where required by law, you may also lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

3. Scope

This Policy applies to personal information we process about you as a user of Towly. It does not apply to third-party websites, apps, or services that we do not control (including Apple, map providers, or cloud hosts), which have their own privacy policies.

4. Information we collect

Depending on how you use the Service, we may process:

  • Account and identity data: email address (including a private relay address if you Hide My Email with Sign in with Apple), Apple Sign In identifier, and display name if you provide one.
  • Location data: precise or approximate device location when you grant permission, used to show nearby reports, warn you near saved places, and tag reports you submit. You can revoke location access in device settings.
  • Reports and community content: incident type, notes, towing company name, photos, parking checks, and related metadata you submit.
  • Saved places and preferences: places you choose to watch and related alert settings.
  • Device and technical data: device type, OS version, app version, language, rough diagnostics needed to run and secure the Service, and — only if you enable notifications — a push notification token.
  • Support communications: messages and related details you send to support.

5. How we collect information

  • Directly from you when you create an account, submit reports, save places, rate parking, or contact support.
  • Automatically from your device when the app runs (technical, diagnostic, and — with permission — location data).
  • From Apple when you Sign in with Apple or when push notifications are delivered.
  • From processors acting on our instructions (for example, cloud database, auth, and object storage).

6. Legal bases (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, we process personal data only where a legal basis applies, including:

  • Contract: to provide the Service you request (account, map, reports, saved places, alerts).
  • Legitimate interests: to secure the Service, prevent abuse, moderate community content, improve reliability, and communicate about service changes — balanced against your rights.
  • Consent: where required (for example, optional push notifications or precise location where consent is required). You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • Legal obligation: where we must retain or disclose information to comply with law.

Where we rely on legitimate interests, you may object as described in “Your rights” below.

7. How we use information

  • Operate the community map and warn drivers about reported predatory-tow areas.
  • Send proximity alerts for places you save (if enabled).
  • Authenticate you and maintain your account.
  • Host, display, and moderate user-submitted reports and parking checks.
  • Respond to support requests and investigate abuse or security issues.
  • Comply with law and enforce our Terms of Use and EULA.

We do not sell your personal information and we do not use it for third-party advertising or cross-context behavioral advertising.

8. What is public

Tow reports and parking checks you submit are shared with the community on the map and feed. Your email address, saved places, and account identifiers are never shown to other users. Do not include private information about yourself or others in public report fields or photos.

9. Where data is stored and international transfers

Your structured data is stored in our managed backend (Supabase). Report photos may be stored with cloud object storage (for example Cloudflare R2) and served through time-limited signed links. Some processing occurs on your device.

Because Towly may be used worldwide, personal data may be processed in countries other than where you live, including the United States and other locations where our providers operate. Those countries may have different data-protection laws than your home country.

For transfers from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland to countries not deemed adequate, we rely on appropriate safeguards where required — such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), the UK International Data Transfer Agreement / Addendum, and provider certifications or contractual commitments — together with technical and organizational measures. You may contact us for more information about transfers applicable to your account.

10. Sharing and processors

We share personal information only as needed to run the Service:

  • Apple — Sign in with Apple and push delivery.
  • Supabase — database, authentication-related backend, and server functions.
  • Cloudflare (or similar) — object storage for report photos.
  • Professional advisors or authorities — only if required by law or to protect rights, safety, or the Service.

These parties act as processors or independent controllers as applicable under their terms. We require processors who handle personal data on our behalf to protect it and use it only for instructed purposes. We do not sell or rent your personal information.

11. Retention and deletion

We retain personal information for as long as your account remains active and as needed to provide the Service. You can delete your account at any time from Settings → Delete My Account. This permanently removes your account and private data (saved places, profile). Reports and parking checks you contributed may remain on the community map in anonymized form (no longer linked to your account), except where law requires otherwise or you validly exercise an erasure right that requires removal of that content.

Backups may persist for a short additional period before being overwritten. Device-local copies remain until you remove the app or clear local data. To request deletion by email, contact support@towly.app.

12. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal information, including access controls, encryption in transit where applicable, and provider security features. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. You are responsible for keeping your device and Apple ID secure. To the maximum extent permitted by law, we are not liable for unauthorized access resulting from circumstances beyond our reasonable control.

13. Your rights (EEA, UK, Switzerland — GDPR)

Subject to applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access your personal data.
  • Rectify inaccurate data.
  • Erase data (“right to be forgotten”).
  • Restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Data portability.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is consent-based.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in your country of residence or work.

You can exercise many of these rights in the app (edit data; revoke location/notifications; delete your account). For other requests, email support@towly.app. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request. Some rights are limited where we have overriding legitimate grounds or legal duties. We aim to respond to GDPR requests within one month (extendable where permitted by law for complex requests).

14. Your rights (California and other US states)

If you are a California resident (and where similar US state laws apply), you may have rights to know/access, delete, correct, opt out of “sale” or “sharing” for cross-context behavioral advertising (we do not sell or share for such purposes), limit use of sensitive personal information where applicable, and non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights.

To submit a request, email support@towly.app or use in-app account deletion.

15. Your choices

  • Revoke location access in iOS Settings.
  • Disable push notifications in iOS Settings.
  • Delete your account and associated private cloud data from Settings.
  • Contact us to request access, correction, or deletion where the app does not already provide it.

Sign in with Apple options (including Hide My Email) are controlled by Apple.

16. Children’s privacy

Towly is not directed to children under 13 (or under 16 in the EEA/UK where a higher digital consent age applies), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, contact support@towly.app and we will take steps to delete it.

17. Cookies, analytics, and tracking

The native iOS app does not use third-party advertising trackers. Our legal web pages may use strictly necessary cookies or similar technologies to operate the site. We do not currently use third-party advertising analytics SDKs in the app. If that changes, we will update this Policy and obtain consent where required.

18. Automated decision-making

Towly does not make solely automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about you. Community reports and alerts are informational tools; you decide how to use them.

19. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top reflects the latest version. Material changes will be indicated by updating that date and, where appropriate, by in-app notice. Continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy, except where applicable law requires additional consent.

20. Contact

Privacy questions or GDPR requests: support@towly.app.
